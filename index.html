This perspective encourages thinking and making grounded in cultural and indigenous practices, bringing depth to your prompt and solution.

Jugaad exemplifies ingenuity that emerges from within specific cultural contexts, often as a response to resource constraints. It embodies the creative spirit that transforms limitations into opportunities—prioritizing local knowledge, available resources, and contextual problem-solving. This anti-colonial and anti-capitalist practice provides a lens for understanding how different approaches to innovation reflect varying cultural values and material conditions.

How might responsible AI development move beyond monolithic approaches to embrace regional cultures, practices, and behaviors in human-centered design?

This research uses "jugaad"—an Indian innovation methodology—as a framework to examine how AI excludes indigenous problem-solving. Through comparative analysis of human vs. AI responses to identical challenges, this work reveals patterns of technological colonialism.

Concept and design by Nidhi Singh Rathore; Based on research by Nidhi Singh Rathore & Shreya Thakkar, from Jugaad to Generative AI: A Framework for Culturally Responsive Human-Centered AI. For more information about the research, visit: anotherism.cargo.site
